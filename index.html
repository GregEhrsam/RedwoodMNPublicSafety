A Foundation at Risk: The 2013 CAD/RMS

The county's core command and control software was implemented over a decade ago. It predates the modern era of public safety technology, lacking the cloud-native architecture, integrated evidence platforms, and AI-driven analytics that are now industry standard. This age represents the single greatest technological vulnerability for the county.

Critical Opportunity Gaps

License Plate Recognition (LPR)

There is currently no LPR technology deployed by local agencies in Redwood County. This is a significant gap, especially for combating drug trafficking (a key community concern) and improving traffic safety on rural roads.

Fire Service Technology

The county's 12 volunteer fire departments are technologically underserved. They lack modern tools like a Fire RMS for automated reporting and mobile apps for faster, safer, and more informed emergency response.

Law Enforcement Staffing & Force Multipliers

With lean departments covering a large rural area, technology is not a luxury‚Äîit's a critical force multiplier. Modern tools automate tasks and provide crucial intelligence, allowing each officer to be more effective and safer in the field.

The Path Forward: A Phased Modernization

A strategic, multi-phase approach allows Redwood County to modernize its entire public safety ecosystem, aligning with its capital planning process and delivering immediate value at each step.

Phase 1

Modernize the Core

Replace the 2013 LETG system with an integrated CommandCentral suite (911, CAD, RMS, Evidence). This fixes the foundation and immediately solves the video evidence problem.

Phase 2

Fill Critical Gaps

Deploy high-impact technologies like LPR to fight drug crime and introduce a Fire RMS and mobile responder apps to empower the 12 volunteer fire departments.

Phase 3

Unify the Ecosystem

Standardize on a single video evidence platform (V300 BWC, M500 In-Car) to create a seamless, end-to-end workflow from capture to courtroom, maximizing efficiency.
